Insecure content.

A core problem you own. A point of view. Language that's yours.

2. Apply it across channels.
LinkedIn. Newsletter. SEO/AEO. Same message, different formats.

3. Test formats until something clicks.
Carousels. Vertical video. Original research. Not everything worked. But they kept swinging.

**https://www.linkedin.com/posts/brendanhufford_in-2016-i-was-a-burned-out-teacher-with-activity-7477349079258415105-n458**
In 2016, I was a burned-out teacher with no formal marketing training and a LinkedIn profile that screamed "please don't hire me."

I didn't have a team.

I didn't have a budget.

I didn't have a clue. (still don’t, most days)

Fast forward to today: I run all three pillars of content strategy - virality, discoverability, and relationship - in unison for myself... and most of my clients.

And I still don't have a big team. Or a massive budget. Or endless hours.

Honestly, you don't need all three firing at full capacity at the same time.

But you do need all 3 existing at the same time.

1. Virality Loops (social) gets you noticed. Weeks of shelf life. Low cost. Aim for debate.

2. Discovery Loops SEO/AEO) gets you found. Evergreen. Higher investment. Answer real questions.

3. Relationship Loops (newsletter/long-form) gets you trusted. Months of shelf life. You own the channel. Aim for consumption.

Social makes you show up in the feeds that matter most, reminding them what you do.
Search captures existing demand and pushes to calls / newsletter.
Newsletter builds long-term trust and relationships.

SO many sales calls include a wild moment where they ask me about my “team.”

And I don’t have one.

Just a system that connects the three instead of treating them like separate departments.
