🔥 GOOGLE AI IS CHANGING THE GAME
For years, the goal was simple:
Get to page one of Google.
Not anymore.

New data from Ahrefs found that only 38% of pages cited in Google's AI Overviews are ranking in Google's top 10 search results.
One year ago that number was 76%.

Sooo...
Google's AI is increasingly pulling answers from places that aren't even on page one.

One thing many marketers are seeing work right now is making content look current.

Not just updating the page.
Showing it's current.

Examples:
"Email Deliverability Trends: June 2026"
"What's Working in LinkedIn Ads Right Now? June 2026"
"Best Travel Deals Available Right Now (June 2026)"

If your content clearly signals recency, that's a strong clue that it may still be relevant and this is a HUGE way to show up in LLM answers.

📧 AI IS NOW READING YOUR EMAILS FIRST

Gmail, Apple Intelligence, and Outlook Copilot are all generating AI summaries of emails and email threads.

Apple Intelligence can display AI-generated summaries in the inbox.

Gmail's Gemini summarizes long emails and conversations.

Outlook Copilot summarizes threads, key points, decisions, and action items.
Which means before your subscriber reads your email...

AI and LLMs are looking for LIVE TEXT! 

🤔 WHAT COUNTS AS LIVE TEXT?
This matters more than ever.
Live text is actual HTML text inside your email.
Examples of live text:
• Headlines typed directly into the email
• Body copy
• Product descriptions
• CTA copy
• Hyperlinked text

Examples that are NOT live text:
• Words embedded inside an image
• Hero-image text
• Text baked into a graphic

One of the biggest mistakes marketers make is creating a gorgeous email where the most important message only exists inside an image.
Humans can see it.

AI systems may not interpret it the same way.

The safest move?
Make sure your most important message exists as actual text in the email itself.
Not just inside the creative.
